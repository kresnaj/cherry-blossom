Cherry blossoms, also known as Sakura in Japan, are the delicate pink and white flowers of the cherry tree (genus Prunus). The history of cherry blossoms, or sakura, dates back over a thousand years in Japan. The earliest references to sakura appear in Japanese literature and poetry from the Nara period (710-794). During this time, the cherry tree was already associated with various religious and cultural practices.

In essence, cherry blossoms are much more than beautiful flowers. They embody profound philosophical and cultural meanings, reflecting the transient nature of life, the joy of renewal, and the importance of appreciating the present moment.
